    Krystal Laryea

    Stanford University

    My research draws on cultural and organizational sociology to address a fundamental question: how are communities and institutions built across lines of difference?

    In the face of widespread political polarization, rising inequalities, growing mental health crises, and the decline of liberal democracies, one of the central tasks for sociology is to offer insights on the possibilities for inclusion and integration, cohesion and solidarity without the elimination of difference.

    As an ethnographer, I examine the micro-level, interactional foundations of culture, institutions, and civil society through long-term participant observation in religious communities, universities, and nonprofits. My work aims to deepen our understanding of (1) how people foster commitments across lines of difference; (2) how culture shapes action; and (3) how organizations are constitutive of, and constituted by, their urban and institutional environments.

    My research has been published in American Sociological Review, Mobilization, the Journal of Higher Education, and Global Perspectives. You can download a copy of my curriculum vitae.
