    Bridge: The pursuit of commitment across lines of difference
    My book dissertation explores the process of cultivating civic commitments across lines of difference within an institution that shapes American society: prestigious research universities. Through two ethnographic cases – a progressive nonprofit for marginalized students and a Pentecostal campus ministry – I trace the experiences of students who see themselves as strangers on campus and navigate strangerhood through their participation in communal, identity-based groups.

    Civic identities and participation
    In an article under review (PDF available here), I theorize how communal participation is sustained in the face of change, using the case of a religious community that shifted to a remote format during the coronavirus pandemic and highlighting the process of reframing in driving participation patterns.
    Sep 1, 2019
    How culture shapes action
    I examine how culture and meaning-making shape individual, social, and organizational outcomes in multiple independent and collaborative projects. In my paper, “Semiotic Shifts,” I advance existing debates in cultural sociology by theorizing how strategies of action evolve in the face of semiotic shifts, which occur when people enter institutional contexts and encounter new meanings for their beliefs.
